The Problem

Pharma compliance is manual, slow, and expensive. AI can fix that.

Every pharmaceutical marketing claim needs FDA review before it goes public. That process — Medical, Legal, and Regulatory (MLR) — is where things get stuck. Teams spend weeks flagging violations, documenting evidence, waiting for sign-off.

The industry needed AI that understood regulatory nuance, not just pattern matching. And it needed someone who'd actually been in the MLR room.

The Approach

15 years in pharma gave me the vocabulary. AI gave me the tools.

Domain Immersion

Mapped the entire MLR workflow with reviewers, regulatory leads, and marketing teams. Found where AI could genuinely help and where it would create new problems.

Architecture

Designed a multimodal system using Gemini to analyze text, images, and video against regulatory databases. Built for explainability — every flag shows its reasoning chain.

Claims Detection

Core engine identifies promotional claims, checks against approved language, flags violations, and suggests compliant alternatives. Everything logged for audit trail.

Routing & Workflow

Flagged content routes to the right reviewer with full context pre-populated. The problem wasn't detection — it was what happened after. This is where the cycle collapses.

The Product Suite

Four products. One platform. Production, not pilots.

Every product below is live at Hedgehox or in active development. This isn't a roadmap deck — it's a working system being built in one of the most regulated industries on earth.

  • Claims Detection Engine Production Multimodal AI using Gemini to analyze text, images, and video against FDA-approved promotional language. Every flag is explainable — the system shows its reasoning chain, not just a red or green light. Built from scratch using AI-assisted development: architecture, pipeline design, prompt engineering, deployment.
  • Routing Tool Production Intelligent content routing that sends flagged material to the right MLR reviewer with full context, evidence, and suggested actions pre-populated. The problem wasn't detection — it was what happened after detection. This is where the review cycle actually collapses.
  • Scope Generator Production AI-powered scope generation for pharma project teams. Takes a brief and outputs structured project scope, timeline, and resourcing — eliminating weeks of back-and-forth before a project even starts. Built for finance and operations teams at pharma clients dealing with complex multi-channel campaigns.
  • Pharma OS In Development The full operational layer — connecting content creation, compliance review, routing, and approval into a single AI-powered workflow. The Claims Detector and Routing Tool are components. Pharma OS is the system that runs them end-to-end. Currently in active development with Hedgehox.
How It Was Built

AI-assisted development in a regulated environment.

Every system here was built using AI-assisted development — Claude Code, Codex CLI, multi-model orchestration — with me as architect and director. The hard problems in pharma AI aren't technical. They're workflow problems: how does a reviewer trust an AI flag? What does an explainable decision look like in an FDA context?

Those questions need someone who's sat in the MLR room, not just someone who knows the stack.
